About

Nehru Zoological Park, located in Hyderabad, India, was established in 1959 and named after India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. over 380 acres, it serves as a wildlife sanctuary and is dedicated to conservation, education, and recreation. The park's mission focuses on promoting biodiversity and raising awareness about wildlife preservation. Notable attractions include a diverse range of species, from Indian elephants to exotic birds, as well as a captivating safari experience that allows visitors to observe animals in a more natural habitat. The park also features a children's train and a natural history museum, enhancing its appeal as a family-friendly destination.
